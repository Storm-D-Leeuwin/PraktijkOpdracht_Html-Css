2009-Current

The Stephen Curry era. The era where the warriors won 3 championships, won 73 games in a season and assambled a superteam called by many: "The Justice League", with a starting 5 of Steph Curry, Klay Thompson, Kevin Durant, Andre Iguodala and Demarcus Cousins.

1997-2009

One of the most iconic teams in recent memories led by Baron Davis: "We Believe Warriors", earned their nickname after knocking the first seaded Mavs led by MVP Dirk Nowitzki out of the first round of the playoffs.

1985-1997

One of the most dynamic trio's: Tim Hardaway, Mitch Richmond and Chris Mullin, "Run TMC". Even though they only played a total of 2 seasons together, they terrorized the league with their fast and exciting play of basketbal.

1978-1985

Due of the loss of key players such as Barry, Wilkes and Thurmond to trades and retirements, the Warriors struggled to put a competitive team on the court from 1978 to 1987 after being one of the NBA's dominant teams in the 1960s and most of the 1970s.

1965-1978

1959-1965

In 1959, the team signed draft pick Wilt Chamberlain. Known as "Wilt the Stilt", he led the team in scoring six times, quickly began shattering NBA scoring records and changed the NBA style of play forever.

1946-1962

The Warriors were founded in 1946 as the Philadelphia Warriors, a charter member of the Basketball Association of America. They were owned by Peter A. Tyrrell, who also owned the Philadelphia Rockets of the American Hockey League.
